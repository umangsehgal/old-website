About Me

I am a Masters student at the Information School at the University of Washington, Seattle. At UW, I am specializing in Data Science and Human Computer Interaction. I am a part of the Code and Cognition Lab led by Prof. Andrew Ko. My interests are at the intersection of HCI and Data Science and their application in domains such as programming tools and accessibility. I would enjoy building tools that enable people to learn to code efficiently.

I am also TA-ing for Intro to Data Science. I curate readings, write and evaluate R script submissions and work on curriculum revision with my instructor Greg Nelson. We are passionate about re-inventing the way Data Science is taught in classroom settings. Have a look at our material in my projects below.

Previously, I was a software engineer at Evive Health where I extensively built enterprise applications using Java, Cassandra, Redis and Elastic Search as our Tech Stack. I also served as a release lead during my work tenure at Evive where I practised Agile Methods to the complete deployment cycle.

Check out the other sections of this site or take a look at my CV to know more about my work.
